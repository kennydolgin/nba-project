Interpretation rule

These visuals describe associations, not causal player impact.

The app compares player-season statistical profiles with team win percentage. It can show which kinds of statistical shapes often appear near stronger teams, but it does not prove that a player caused a team to win.

Data scope

What counts as a row?

A player-season row represents a player on a specific team in a specific season. If a player appeared for multiple teams, those team contexts can appear as separate player-team-season rows.

The comparison file used by the app is static/data/player_seasons.csv. It is generated from outputs/tables/top3_h1_player_scatter_data.csv and includes only the fields needed for the interactive site.

Team context

Win percentage and success buckets

win_pct is the team's season win percentage for the player-team-season row. The app uses three ordered buckets: low-winning, middle-winning, and high-winning teams.

Those buckets support the stacked profile chart, color encodings, example selection, and high-winning benchmark bands.

Core calculations

Player role metrics

Metric Meaning in the app Why it is used
points_per_36 Scoring production normalized to 36 minutes. Compares scoring rate without making starters and bench players purely a minutes contest.
points_per_game Traditional scoring volume. Provides the familiar baseline that the project moves beyond.
fga_per_game Shot attempt volume. Separates high-usage scoring roles from lower-shot roles.
efg_pct Effective field-goal percentage. Accounts for the extra value of made three-point shots when comparing shooting efficiency.
assists_per_36 Creation and passing production per 36 minutes. Captures offensive role value that points alone misses.
rebounds_per_36 Rebounding production per 36 minutes. Represents possession-finishing and frontcourt role context.
stocks_per_36 Steals plus blocks per 36 minutes. Summarizes box-score defensive event production.
role_category_count Number of role categories where a player-season stands out. Approximates breadth: broader statistical profiles can differ from narrow scoring-only profiles.

Role labels

Profile groups and examples

profile_group classifies player-seasons into broad role shapes such as balanced, mixed, or narrow. The app then compares how often those profile types appear across team-success buckets.

efficient_lower_shot_label identifies player-seasons that combine strong shooting efficiency with lower shot volume. defensive_event_profile distinguishes steals-plus-blocks leaders from top scorers and overlap cases.

Percentiles

How the fingerprints work

For each metric, the app sorts all reviewed player-seasons and places the selected season into that distribution. A 75th percentile scoring value means that season ranked above roughly three quarters of reviewed player-seasons for that metric.

Turnovers are shown descriptively rather than treated as a value judgment. Higher turnover percentiles usually reflect more on-ball responsibility as well as mistakes.

Interactive comparisons

How similarity and the new visuals are computed

Similar player-seasons

The similarity search compares percentile-ranked values for scoring, assists, rebounds, steals plus blocks, and shooting efficiency. It first tries to compare within the same position group; if that pool is too small, it falls back to the full player-season set. The closest rows are the smallest Euclidean distances across those percentile dimensions.

Winning Profile Galaxy

The galaxy's x-axis averages scoring-rate percentile and shot-volume percentile. Its y-axis averages creation, rebounding, defensive-event, efficiency, and role-breadth percentiles. Color shows team success bucket, and selected players are marked with rings.

Archetype Radar

The radar uses percentile spokes for scoring, creation, rebounding, defensive events, efficiency, and role breadth. The small value strip under the radar keeps exact underlying values visible so the shape is not the only evidence.

Winning Bands

For each metric, the shaded band is the middle 50 percent of high-winning-team player-seasons. Selected player dots show whether a season sits below, inside, or above that descriptive benchmark range.

Summary charts

Bucket shares and scatter plots

The stacked bars calculate shares within each team-success bucket. This prevents high-volume buckets from dominating the display and makes profile mix easier to compare across low-, middle-, and high-winning teams.

The scatter plots show direct bivariate evidence: scoring versus team win percentage, shot volume versus efficiency, and scoring versus steals plus blocks.

Map calculation

State-level team win percentage

The map joins team abbreviations to states. For states with multiple teams in a season, the displayed value is the average win percentage across those teams. States with no NBA team in the selected season are left white.

This map is primarily a D3/Flask course-template connection; the main analytical story is the player role comparison.

Limitations

What the analysis should not claim

The project should use phrases like "associated with winning," "similar historical player-seasons," and "winning-profile context." It should avoid claims that a role or individual player caused winning unless a separate causal design supports that claim.

Important omitted context includes lineup combinations, opponent strength, injuries, coaching strategy, playoff performance, role changes within a season, and non-box-score defense. The visuals are best read as descriptive evidence for player-role context, not final player rankings.
